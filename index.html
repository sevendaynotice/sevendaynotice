New Mexico Tenant Rights: Using a 7-Day Notice

Learn your rights under NM law (Stat § 47-8-27)

What is a 7-Day Notice to Repair?

Under the New Mexico Uniform Owner-Resident Relations Act (NM Stat § 47-8-27), tenants have the right to demand repairs within seven days if their landlord fails to maintain a habitable property.

When Can You Use It?

  • Essential Services: No heat, water, or electricity
  • Health & Safety: Mold, pests, broken locks, unsafe conditions
  • Major Repairs: Plumbing, heating, or structural issues
  • Landlord Violations: Illegal entry, failure to follow lease terms

Understanding Rent Abatement

Rent abatement allows tenants to reduce or withhold rent when their landlord fails to maintain a habitable property.

Partial Abatement

One-third of the daily rent can be deducted for each day that the issue remains unresolved after notice.

Full Abatement

If the unit is uninhabitable and the tenant must vacate, they may withhold all rent until repairs are made.

[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[City, State, ZIP Code]
[Phone Number]
[Email Address]
[Date]

To:
[Landlord’s Name]
[Landlord’s Address]
[City, State, ZIP Code]

Subject: Seven-Day Notice to Repair or Remedy

Dear [Landlord’s Name],

I am writing to formally notify you of the need for repairs at my rental unit located at [Your Rental Address].
Under New Mexico law (NM Stat § 47-8-27), tenants have the right to demand repairs within seven (7) days.

Please confirm in writing when the repairs will be completed.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
